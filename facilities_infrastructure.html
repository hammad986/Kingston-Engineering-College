Smart Classroom

  • The virtual classroom transcends the boundaries of location, time and space providing a flexible learning environment for all.

  • Schools, Universities and corporate organizations benefit from Virtual classrooms because it provides an excellent way for experts to teach a geographically dispersed group of students without hassle.

  • In a cyber practicum, however, the teachers can meet a variety of students - whoever signed up from anywhere around the world.

  • Each student has his or her own account, and the teacher can develop lessons targeting each student's needs.

  • The cyber practicum supervisors review the lessons and the teachers revise them to address their suggestions.

  • Being a premier institute, we have lectures by several senior professors and subject experts from all Indian Institute of Technologies.

  • Providing access to all archived lectures was proving to be a challenge and so we were looking for an E-learning solution that would provide benefits beyond the classroom environment.

  • The archived classroom sessions are presented to the students via the Video Portal, a Web-based component of the Digital Media System that allows students to quickly browse, search and view digital media interactively.

Canteen

Kingston Engineering College provides a well-maintained and hygienic canteen facility within the campus, serving a wide variety of freshly prepared nutritious meals, snacks, and beverages to students and staff throughout the day. The canteen operates under strict hygiene standards ensuring a safe and healthy dining experience for the entire campus community.

The canteen menu includes a diverse range of South Indian, North Indian, and continental foods catering to the varied tastes and dietary preferences of students from different parts of the country. Special provisions are made for students with dietary restrictions, and fresh juices, snacks, and desserts are available at affordable rates throughout the day.

The spacious seating area offers a comfortable and refreshing environment for students to dine and relax between their academic schedules. Regular quality audits and feedback mechanisms are in place to continuously improve the services, ensuring maximum satisfaction for the college's growing student population.

Mess

The Mess facility at Kingston Engineering College is dedicated to providing wholesome home-style meals to hostel residents and day scholars. Operating multiple sessions throughout the day for breakfast, lunch, and dinner, the mess ensures that students receive balanced and nutritious food at highly subsidized and affordable rates, without compromising on quality.

Our mess operates under the supervision of qualified catering staff who prioritize cleanliness, taste, and nutrition in every meal served. The weekly rotating menu is thoughtfully planned by a dietician to include essential nutrients, seasonal vegetables, proteins, and balanced carbohydrates, ensuring the health and well-being of all students residing in the campus hostels.

The dining hall is equipped with efficient service counters and ample seating capacity to accommodate a large number of students simultaneously. Regular feedback is collected from mess committee members and students to improve the quality, variety, and overall dining experience throughout the academic year.

Water Conservation

Rain water harvesting pits have been established across the campus to recharge groundwater and reduce surface run-off effectively.

Sensor-based taps and automated drip irrigation systems have been installed to minimize water wastage in all campus buildings and gardens.

Treated wastewater from the campus sewage treatment plant is reused for irrigation of lawns and gardens throughout the college premises.

Awareness campaigns and workshops on water conservation practices are conducted regularly for students, staff, and campus residents.

Multiple water storage tanks and underground sumps have been constructed to ensure adequate water supply even during dry seasons.

The institution has achieved significant reduction in per capita water consumption due to systematic conservation initiatives adopted campus-wide.
